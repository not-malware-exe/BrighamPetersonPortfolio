Description

Void Directive is a 2d Metroidvania that will be set in a post-apocalyptic world that is populated by zombie like husks that are made of flesh and circuitry. The husks and most machinery are controlled nodes in a cybernetic hivemind that is orchestrated by a singular rouge AI. You, as the player, are one of the last humans left in the overgrown remains of a once thriving megalopolis. At the start of the game, you live on the outskirts, avoiding detection from the husks that patrol the area, and the cameras and drones that survey the region. If you are detected, all enemies (husks, drones, etc.) in the local region will know of your position and will attempt to infect or kill you. To avoid detection or give yourself a fighting chance, you will have gear and guns to help you survive. The guns you have aren't bound to realistic physics, their recoil can be used to platform and keep a distance from the enemy. In combat, you can use the guns not only to hurt enemies but also to move around and dodge.

As of current, I only have most of the core systems implemented and very few items implemented. I have dummy enemies, that sit still and do nothing but drop items with the idName 'scrap'. I have an untextured shotgun that can reload and shoot, with the recoil that can be used as a speed boost or mid-air jump. I have made a save system that loads data from a json file, and most of the inventory (excluding ui) implemented. I have level transitions and smooth camera movement.
